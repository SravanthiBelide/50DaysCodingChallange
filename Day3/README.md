## **The Healthy Kids Nutrition \& Growth Analysis**







##### Objective:





\- To analyze child health data and identify correlations between food habits, lifestyle behaviors, and growth outcomes (BMI, health status).

\- To provide actionable recommendations for the “Healthy Fut





##### Dataset:

• ChildID: Unique identifier for each child. 

• Age\_Months: The child's age in months. 

• Gender: The child's gender (Male/Female). 

• Height\_cm: The child's height in centimeters. 

• Weight\_kg: The child's weight in kilograms. 

• Daily\_Fruit\_Servings: Average number of fruit servings per day. 

• Daily\_Veggie\_Servings: Average number of vegetable servings per day. 

• Daily\_Sugar\_Drinks: Average number of sugary drinks per day. 

• ScreenTime\_Hours: Average daily screen time in hours. 

• Region: The geographic region where the child lives.

###### Key Findings:



\- High sugary drink consumption is linked to higher BMI, especially in South and East regions.

\- Longer screen time (>2 hours/day) correlates with elevated BMI.

\- Higher fruit and vegetable intake supports healthier weight ranges.

\- Underweight cases are more common in East and North regions with low fruit/veggie intake.



###### Recommendations:



\- Reduce sugary drink consumption through awareness campaigns.

\- Promote fruit and vegetable intake via school nutrition programs.

\- Encourage active play and reduce screen time through community initiatives



###### Deliverables:



\- Dashboards: Visual summaries of BMI and health status.

\- Pivot Tables: Correlation analysis of habits vs. growth outcomes.

\- Summary Sheet: Executive summary with findings and recommendations.
